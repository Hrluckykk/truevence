BGV MIGRATION GUIDE

How to Switch Your Background Verification Vendor Smoothly

Changing your background verification provider can feel daunting, but with the right plan it’s seamless. This guide covers how to evaluate a new BGV partner, migrate data securely, and ensure zero disruption to your hiring process.

📅 July 2026 ⏱ 9 min read 👤 Truevence Editorial Team

Why Organisations Switch BGV Providers

HR teams decide to change their background verification company for various reasons. Recognising these triggers helps you confirm the decision and justify the move internally.

  • • Slow turnaround times causing delays in joining dates
  • • Inconsistent or inaccurate reports
  • • Poor candidate experience or lack of self‑service portal
  • • Limited coverage – vendor can't verify across multiple states
  • • Hidden costs or rigid pricing models
  • • Data security and compliance concerns

Signs It’s Time to Move On

🔴 Red Flags with Your Current Vendor

  • • No real‑time tracking dashboard
  • • Frequent errors in reports
  • • Poor communication & support
  • • Not scalable for new hiring needs

🟢 What a Modern BGV Partner Should Offer

  • • Digital + physical hybrid verification
  • • Automated candidate consent workflows
  • • Pan‑India coverage with fast TAT
  • • Transparent per‑check pricing

Step‑by‑Step Transition Plan

1

Audit Your Current BGV Process

Document the exact checks you use, volumes, pain points, and integration requirements. This becomes your evaluation criteria.

2

Shortlist New Vendors

Look at top BGV companies in India and compare them based on your audit criteria. Request demos and trial reports.

3

Check Contract & Notice Period

Review your existing agreement. Most BGV contracts have a 30‑day notice period. Align the switch date accordingly.

4

Data Migration & Security

Ensure the new vendor has secure data migration protocols. Transfer historical reports and candidate data with encryption and consent.

5

Run a Pilot Before Full Switch

Start with a small batch of verifications through the new vendor while wrapping up pending checks with the old one. This parallel run de‑risks the transition.

6

Inform Candidates & Stakeholders

Send a clear communication to candidates about the new verification link and portal. Train your HR team on the new platform.

Common Mistakes to Avoid During Migration

  • ✗ Not auditing your existing data for accuracy before migration
  • ✗ Cancelling old contract before new vendor is fully operational
  • ✗ Forgetting to update HR policies and offer letters with new vendor details
  • ✗ Skipping the pilot phase due to time pressure

Questions to Ask a Prospective BGV Partner

  • • How do you handle data migration from a previous vendor?
  • • What’s your average turnaround time for employment & education checks?
  • • Can we run a pilot with 10‑15 test verifications?
  • • How do you ensure business continuity during the switch?
  • • Do you provide a dedicated transition manager?
