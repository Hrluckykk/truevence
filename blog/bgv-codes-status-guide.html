Understanding BGV Codes: Status, Report & Verification Result Codes

Background verification reports often use specific codes or status tags like "Green," "Yellow," or "Red." This guide decodes the most common BGV codes, helping HR teams interpret results accurately and make informed hiring decisions.

📅 July 2026 ⏱ 8 min read 👤 Truevence Editorial Team

What Are BGV Codes?

BGV codes are standardized result indicators used by background verification companies to communicate the outcome of each check. They simplify complex verification data into easily understandable statuses, allowing HR teams to quickly assess a candidate's clearance level without reading through lengthy reports.

While terminology may vary slightly between vendors, most BGV providers in India use a color‑coded system (green, amber, red) or numeric/code‑based results (e.g., "Clear," "Minor Discrepancy," "Adverse"). Understanding these codes is critical to maintaining consistency and fairness in your hiring process.

The Color Code System in BGV Reports

✓

Green

Clear / Verified

All details match; no discrepancies found. Candidate is cleared for onboarding.

!

Amber / Yellow

Partial Discrepancy

Minor inconsistencies found; may need additional review or candidate clarification.

✗

Red

Adverse / Not Verified

Major discrepancy or fraud detected. Further investigation or rejection may be warranted.

Codes Specific to Verification Types

Some BGV reports include check‑type specific codes that provide granular insight.

Identity Check Codes

  • • ID-VERIFIED: Aadhaar/PAN confirmed via UIDAI/NSDL
  • • ID-MISMATCH: Name/DOB doesn't match official records
  • • ID-FAKE: Document found to be forged

Education Check Codes

  • • EDU-VERIFIED: Degree confirmed directly with university
  • • EDU-NOTFOUND: University has no record
  • • EDU-INCOMPLETE: Course not completed

Criminal Check Codes

  • • CRIM-CLEAR: No adverse record found
  • • CRIM-FLAGGED: Possible match; under review
  • • CRIM-CONFIRMED: Criminal record confirmed

How to Use BGV Codes in Your Hiring Workflow

Establish an internal policy that clearly defines which status codes automatically clear a candidate, which require review, and which lead to rejection. This ensures fairness and consistency.

  • ✓ Auto‑clear all "Green" reports for faster onboarding
  • ✓ Review "Amber" cases with the candidate and hiring manager
  • ✓ Escalate "Red" cases to a committee for final decision

Why Standardised BGV Codes Matter

Using clear, consistent verification codes eliminates confusion, reduces bias, and helps you scale your hiring without second‑guessing every report. It also makes vendor transitions easier, as you can map old vendor codes to new ones.
